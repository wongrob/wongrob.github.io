My Introduction to "Animanga"

I only got into anime and Japanese pop culture when the pandemic hit in 2020, but I soon got hooked on it and have continued consuming it ever since. I have been around Japanese culture since I am half Japanese, but never really got into the pop culture part until then. The first anime that really got me into anime was Sword Art Online, which I loved because of the overall concept of being trapped in virtual reality, and I actually liked the main character, mostly because I wished I could be him. I'm not a fan of the second season (of the anime), but I think it has gotten a lot better with the Alicization arc, and I also think that the Progressive series is really good. But I have continued to watch anime and have been discovering what I like and don't like. I have learned that I love romance, especially bittersweet romance, which is surprising because I am not a fan of live-action romance because I think it is too cheesy. I am also a sucker for slice of life anime, but I consider this more of a format for anime more so than a genre.

I also really love reading, so of course I had to get into light novels. I think technically Oregairu is the first light novel series I started reading, which I kind of regret because I read it after the anime and felt like I didn't get much from it. However, I ended up also reading the Sword Art Online series and ended up really enjoying it also getting me hooked on light novels, especially the Progressive series. I've started and continue to only read the light novels for animes that I have watched and liked because I think there is a lower risk of not enjoying the book that way, which I don't like taking too much risk with books I don't like, mostly because I am a slow reader and it takes me a long time, and my perfectionism makes me feel like I have to finish the book (although maybe not the whole series) even when I'm not enjoying it. The Rascal Does Not Dream series was a bit of a risk because I was worried about the title, but I ended up really enjoying it and it was proof that you can't always judge a book by its cover (or title). There are so many more light novels I want to read, and am slowly catching up on, but it is hard to balance them with other books I want to read.

Finally there is manga. Personally I am a fan of reading light novels over manga, mostly because I'm not a big fan of "reading pictures". I feel like I prefer to read words, so I prefer light novels, or if I want to see pictures, I would rather watch anime, so manga is hard for me to get into. However, I have read manga for anime series I really like. I started with Fruits Basket because the books looked so cool, but as I went through the manga, that was when I realized I wasn't really a fan of manga. But there were some animes I enjoyed so much that I wanted to read the manga, such as Your Lie in April, and so I ended up doing that and enjoyed the manga. Now I also only read manga for animes I really enjoyed. However, I am starting to think that I should give manga another shot, especially since I read it a lot faster and there are a lot of good stories that are only in manga and not anime or light novels. So, we'll see how that goes.

That is my not so brief introduction to my interest in anime, manga, and light novels. Anyways, this page is dedicated to my thoughts on the anime, manga, and light novels I have seen/read. I'll probably also add lists of to watch/read as well. I may add some more details for specific series, but for now I am going to keep it simple with these lists and some summarized thoughts in a table. I also wanted to make this to learn how to use a spreadsheet to create an automatically updating page.
